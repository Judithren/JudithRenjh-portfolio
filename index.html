My
Reflection

To be honest, when I first started taking DT classes, I had no idea what I was doing. But looking back now, it's unbelievable that I actually made these things.

"

My deepest memory is not how perfect a project was, but those nights when I stayed up late with friends. When doing DT homework, we often stay late in the classroom, chatting while doing things, and sometimes we even order takeout together. Once we were doing Endanzoo, we tried many times to adjust that mechanism, but it didn't work. Finally, we succeeded before curling, and everyone was very excited. Although I was very tired during class the next day, the feeling of solving problems together was great.

DT taught me how to turn ideas into real things instead of just staying on paper. I have also learned that failure is nothing, because every time you fail, you will know how to do better next time. However, DT does owe me a lot of sleep, and it is responsible for this.

These projects are proof of the time I spent with my friends.
